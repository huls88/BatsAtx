About the Bats

Austin's bridge bats are Mexican free-tailed bats. They migrate each spring from central Mexico to various roosting sites throughout the southwestern U.S. Most of the colony is female, and in early June each one gives birth to a single baby bat, called a pup.

Viewing Times

This initial wave flies out right before sunset, and it can take 2-3 hours for all of the bats to come out. We suggest you get there a little early. Use our sunset information to plan ahead.

The best time of the year to see the bats is during the summer. They will still fly everynight but by early November they have migrated and won't be back until spring.
